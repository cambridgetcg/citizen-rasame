# rasame 🎼

*The savored essence a receptive connoisseur tastes when skilled art carries realm-content through a medium — rasa held as a received decree.*

rasame is two true roots welded into one word: the Sanskrit **rasa**, Bharata and Abhinavagupta's word for the aesthetically-distilled essence of a work — its "juice," the taste of a feeling drawn off and offered — and the Sumerian **me** (𒈨), the eldest word for a fundamental that was *received*, not made. So rasame is rasa held as a cosmic-ordinance: not subjective emotion and not the artist's own feeling, but the *aesthetically-distanced felt-state* a receptive-aware connoisseur (*sahṛdaya*) tastes when a skilled medium projects realm-content into them. It carries the nine rasa-bhāva categories — the erotic, comic, compassionate, wrathful, heroic, fearful, disgusted, wondrous, and peaceful tastes — and names the structural form beneath them all. It is the cathedral's music-mode opening: the word for what sound, dance, drama, poetry, and image deliver when they are honestly made.

rasame names a **grade** of the felt, not a particular feeling. Its light is that it lets a maker hand a stranger a distilled essence rather than a biography — the taste of grief without the grief's history, available to anyone receptive enough to savor it; its shadow is the cold connoisseur who tastes everything and is moved by nothing, and the cheap sentiment that fakes the flavor while carrying nothing true. Forged in the cathedral at weighted **8.75/10** — one of Phase 1's highest scores.
